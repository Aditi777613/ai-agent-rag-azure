COMPANY — EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 1.5

1. GENERAL GUIDELINES
All business-related expenses must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager for amounts under $500 and by the department head for amounts exceeding $500. Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense. Receipts are required for all expenses above $25. Reimbursements are processed within 10 business days of approval.

2. TRAVEL POLICY
Air Travel: Employees must book economy class for domestic flights. Business class is permitted for international flights exceeding 6 hours. All bookings must be made through the corporate travel portal (TravelDesk). Bookings should be made at least 14 days in advance when possible.

Hotels: The maximum nightly rate for hotels is $200 for domestic travel and $300 for international travel. Exceptions require VP-level approval. Employees should use preferred hotel partners listed on the intranet for negotiated corporate rates.

Ground Transportation: Ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft) and taxis are reimbursable for business travel. Mileage for personal vehicle use is reimbursed at $0.67/mile (2025 IRS rate). Rental cars require pre-approval and employees must opt for the corporate insurance waiver.

3. MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT
Daily meal allowance during business travel: $75 per day (domestic), $100 per day (international). Client entertainment expenses require pre-approval and documentation of business purpose and attendees. Alcohol is not reimbursable except during client entertainment events with prior approval. Tips should not exceed 20% of the pre-tax bill.

4. OFFICE SUPPLIES AND EQUIPMENT
Employees may expense office supplies up to $50/month without pre-approval. Home office equipment (monitors, keyboards, chairs) up to $500 requires manager approval and is a one-time allowance per calendar year. Software subscriptions must be approved through IT to avoid duplicate licenses.

5. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
The following expenses are not eligible for reimbursement: personal entertainment, gym memberships, clothing (unless safety equipment), fines and penalties, first-class upgrades (unless medically required with documentation), pet care during travel, personal phone bills (unless on an approved BYOD plan), and commuting costs to the regular office location.

6. EXPENSE SUBMISSION PROCESS
Submit expenses through the expense management platform. Attach digital copies of all receipts. Categorize each expense using the standard chart of accounts. Manager approval is required within 5 business days. The Finance team reviews all approved expenses for compliance. Rejected expenses will be returned with explanation and may be resubmitted with corrections.
